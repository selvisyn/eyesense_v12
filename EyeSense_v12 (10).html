📖 How to Use EyeSense

1
Choose your input mode
On the start screen, pick Eye Gaze for hands-free use, Touch for finger control, or Both for combined input. Eye Gaze requires calibration.
👁 Eye · ☝️ Touch · 🤝 Both
2
Calibrate (Eye Gaze only)
17 dots will appear on screen one by one. Look steadily at each dot until the ring fills. Stay still — the system samples your iris position to build a personal model. Takes about 45 seconds.
🎯 ~45 seconds · 17 points
3
Dwell to select
Look at any button and hold your gaze steady. A yellow bar fills from the bottom — when it reaches 100% the item is selected. Dwell time is adjustable (default 1.2s).
⏱ Gaze + hold = select
4
Single blink = quick select
Enable Single Blink Select in Accessibility settings. Then, when you are looking at an item with at least 8% dwell, a single blink instantly selects it — much faster than waiting for the full dwell.
😉 Blink = select (optional)
5
Double blink = go back
Blink twice quickly (within 480ms) to close the current panel and return to the previous screen. Works everywhere — no settings needed.
😉😉 Double blink = back
6
Iris scroll inside panels
When a panel (Phrase Board, Accessibility, Smart Home, etc.) is open, move your gaze to the top or bottom edge of that panel to scroll. The further into the edge zone, the faster it scrolls.
↕ Top/bottom edge = scroll
7
Phrase Board
Dwell on a phrase card to speak it aloud. Tap + to add your own phrases with a custom emoji. The system remembers your most-used phrases and shows them first.
💬 Gaze → dwell → speaks
8
Keyboard
Dwell on each key to type. Word suggestions appear at the top — dwell on a suggestion to complete the word. Press 🔊 to read your text aloud. Use 🎤 for voice dictation.
⌨️ Dwell-type · suggestions · TTS
9
Online learning
Every time you dwell-select something, the system silently refines your gaze model. After 8 new interactions a small model update runs — accuracy improves over time without recalibration.
🔄 Auto-improves with use
10
Tips for best accuracy
• Sit 40–55 cm from screen · • Good lighting on your face · • Keep head relatively still · • Recalibrate if you move to a different device or change seating position · • Use Accuracy Test to check your current error.
💡 Distance · Light · Stillness

GAZE ESTIMATION — MATH
Iris offset (normalised gaze vector):
 rOx = (iris_x − eye_left_x) / eye_width − 0.5
 rOy = (iris_y − eye_top_y) / eye_height − 0.5
Feature vector (10-dim): f = [rOx, rOy, lOx, lOy, avg_x, avg_y, nX, nY, Δx, Δy]
Poly expand (degree-2): f' = [f, cross-terms] → 20 dims total
Ridge fit: w = (XᵀX + λI)⁻¹ Xᵀy, λ=0.001, fitted for modelX and modelY
Predict: screen_x = w⃗·[1, f']   (predict() <1ms)
Head comp: tx += yaw × W × 0.06   ty += pitch × H × 0.045
Online update: blend(0.85·w_old + 0.15·w_new) after 8 dwell events
VALIDATION & ACCURACY
Approach: Personalised calibration + Online Learning (v12)
Each user builds their own model via 17-point calibration (ridge regression, λ=0.001). The model continuously improves during use via online learning: every 8 dwell selections trigger a weighted model blend (85% old + 15% new).

Key improvements in v12:
· Online learning → accuracy improves with usage, no recalibration needed
· Interim model updates during calibration → live error feedback per point
· Single blink = select, double blink = back — universal across all panels
· Iris scroll — gaze top/bottom 14% of screen scrolls active container

Limitation: Cross-user generalisability not tested. Each user must complete their own calibration.

Pilot data (n=3, laptop, normal light, 40–55 cm):
Avg. error ≈ 1.8 cm · p90 ≈ 2.7 cm · Pipeline ≈ 75 ms
Full study plan: 10–15 participants · laptop + smartphone · low/medium/bright · static + dynamic head positions.
SECURITY & PRIVACY
Camera access: WebRTC getUserMedia — browser permission dialog required on each origin.
Frame storage: Zero. Frames pass to FaceMesh and are immediately discarded.
Memory: Temporary pixel buffer (120×68 canvas) for luma sampling only.
Persistence: Only calibration model weights (modelX/Y) stored in localStorage — no biometric data.
Permission lifecycle: Camera stream released on page unload via stream.getTracks().forEach(t→t.stop()).
